Pasta Aglio e Olio

Description

Pasta Aglio e Olio, meaning "garlic and oil" in Italian, is a simple yet flavorful dish originating from Naples. This recipe highlights the magic of just a few pantry staples: olive oil, garlic, chili flakes, and spaghetti. Itâ€™s light, comforting, and perfect for a quick lunch or dinner.

Ingredients:

  • 200g of spaghetti or any pasta of choice
  • 4-5 garlic cloves, thinly sliced
  • 1/4 cup of extra virgin olive oil
  • 1 tsp red chili flakes
  • 2 tbsp fresh parsley, chopped
  • Freshly Ground pepper
  • Grated Parmesan Cheese
  • Salt to Taste

Steps:

  1. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Add the spaghetti and cook until al dente according to package instructions. Reserve 1/2 cup of pasta water before draining.
  2. Heat the olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add the sliced garlic and cook gently until golden and fragrant. Be careful not to burn the garlic, as it will turn bitter.
  3. Stir in the chili flakes and cook for another 30 seconds.
  4. Add the drained spaghetti to the skillet and toss to coat in the garlic oil.
  5. If the pasta seems dry, add a splash of the reserved pasta water to loosen it up.
  6. Sprinkle with salt, freshly ground black pepper, and parsley (if using). Toss well.
  7. Serve immediately, garnished with Parmesan cheese if desired.
