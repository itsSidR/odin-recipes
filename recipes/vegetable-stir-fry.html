Vegetable Stir-Fry

Description

Vegetable Stir-Fry is a colorful mix of veggies cooked quickly in a savory sauce, making it both delicious and nutritious. This dish is a staple in Asian cuisine, offering a balance of crunchy textures and bold flavors. It can be enjoyed on its own or served over steamed rice or noodles.

Ingredients:

  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil (or sesame oil for more flavor)
  • 1 small onion, sliced
  • 2-3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1-inch piece of ginger, grated
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 cup broccoli florets
  • 1 medium carrot, julienned or thinly sliced
  • 1 cup snap peas or green beans
  • 1/2 cup mushrooms, sliced (optional)
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp oyster sauce (or hoisin sauce for a vegetarian option)
  • 1 tsp sesame oil
  • 1 tsp cornstarch dissolved in 2 tbsp water (for thickening)
  • 1 tsp sugar (optional)
  • Sesame seeds
  • Chopped green onions

Steps:

  1. Prepare the Sauce:In a small bowl, mix soy sauce, oyster sauce, sesame oil, cornstarch slurry, and sugar. Set aside.
  2. Cook the Vegetables:Heat the vegetable oil in a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat. Add the garlic, ginger, and onion, and saut√© for 1-2 minutes until fragrant.
  3. Add the broccoli, carrots, and bell peppers. Stir-fry for 3-4 minutes, keeping the veggies crisp and colorful.
  4. Add the snap peas, mushrooms, or any other quick-cooking vegetables. Stir-fry for another 2-3 minutes.
  5. Add the Sauce:Pour the prepared sauce over the vegetables and toss to coat evenly. Cook for 1-2 minutes until the sauce thickens slightly.
  6. TServe:ransfer to a serving dish and garnish with sesame seeds or chopped green onions. Serve hot with steamed rice or noodles.
