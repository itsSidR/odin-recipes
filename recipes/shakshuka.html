Shakshuka

Description

Shakshuka is a hearty one-pan dish made with eggs poached in a spiced tomato and bell pepper sauce. Popular as a breakfast or brunch item, it’s packed with flavors from cumin, paprika, and fresh herbs. It’s easy to make and pairs wonderfully with crusty bread for dipping.

Ingredients:

  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 4-5 medium tomatoes, chopped (or one 14-oz can of crushed tomatoes)
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1 tsp paprika
  • 1/4 tsp red chili flakes (optional, for heat)
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 4-6 eggs
  • Fresh parsley or cilantro, chopped (for garnish)
  • Optional: feta cheese crumbles

Steps:

  1. Make the Base:Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add the onion and cook until softened, about 3-4 minutes.
  2. Add the bell pepper and garlic, and sauté for another 2-3 minutes until fragrant.
  3. Cook the Tomato Sauce:Stir in the cumin, paprika, and chili flakes. Cook for 30 seconds to release the spices' aroma.
  4. Add the tomatoes (fresh or canned) and season with salt and pepper. Simmer the mixture for 10-12 minutes, stirring occasionally, until it thickens slightly.
  5. Poach the Eggs:Use a spoon to make small wells in the sauce and crack an egg into each well.
  6. Cover the skillet and let the eggs cook for 5-7 minutes, or until the whites are set but the yolks are still runny (cook longer if you prefer firmer yolks).
  7. Garnish and Serve:Sprinkle with fresh parsley or cilantro, and crumble some feta cheese on top if desired.
  8. Serve hot with crusty bread, pita, or even over rice.
